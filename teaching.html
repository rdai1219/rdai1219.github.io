Teaching

Educating the next generation of biostatisticians and data scientists

Courses Taught

BIOS 925: Theory of General Linear and Mixed Models in Biostatistics

3 credit hours

Graduate Course

Spring 2025

Advanced theoretical foundations of linear models and mixed-effects models, with applications to biomedical research. Topics include generalized linear models, variance components, and longitudinal data analysis.

BIOS 939: High Dimensional Inference and Multiple Testing

3 credit hours

Graduate Course

Fall 2023

Statistical methods for analyzing high-dimensional biomedical data. Topics include false discovery rate control, multiple testing procedures, variable selection, and dimension reduction techniques.

Course Developer

BIOS 818: Biostatistical Linear Models: Methods and Application

3 credit hours

Graduate Course

Fall 2022, Fall 2024

Application-focused course on linear regression methods for biomedical research. Covers model building, diagnostics, inference, and interpretation using real-world health data.

BIOS 808: Biostatistics II

3 credit hours

Graduate Course

Spring 2021, Spring 2022

Intermediate biostatistics course covering ANOVA, linear regression, logistic regression, and survival analysis with emphasis on public health applications.
